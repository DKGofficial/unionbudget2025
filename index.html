India's Union Budget 2025 Highlights

On February 1, 2025, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman presented the Union Budget, focusing on stimulating economic growth, providing tax relief to the middle class, and enhancing agricultural productivity.

Taxation Reforms

  • Income Tax Relief: The tax exemption threshold has been increased, providing significant relief to the salaried middle class. This move is expected to boost household consumption and savings. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}
  • TDS and TCS Rationalization: The limit for tax deduction at source (TDS) on interest for senior citizens has been doubled from ₹50,000 to ₹1 lakh, and the annual limit for TDS on rent increased from ₹2.4 lakh to ₹6 lakh. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Agriculture and Rural Development

  • Prime Minister Dhan-Dhaanya Krishi Yojana: A new scheme introduced to enhance agricultural productivity and support farmers. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • Enhanced Credit through Kisan Credit Card (KCC): Facilitating short-term loans for 7.7 crore farmers, fishermen, and dairy farmers with an enhanced loan limit of ₹5 lakh. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Pulse Production Mission: Launching a six-year mission focusing on increasing the production of pulses like Tur, Urad, and Masoor to reduce import dependence. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Establishment of a Makhana Board in Bihar: Aimed at promoting the cultivation and marketing of Makhana, benefiting local farmers. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Infrastructure and Capital Expenditure

  • Increased Capital Expenditure: The government plans to increase its capital expenditure to ₹11-11.5 lakh crore in FY26, focusing on infrastructure projects such as roads, water supply systems, metro systems, railways, defense, digital infrastructure, and green technologies. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Science and Innovation

  • Research, Development & Innovation Initiative: An allocation of ₹20,000 crore has been made to implement a private sector-driven research, development, and innovation initiative. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  • PM Research Fellowship: Provision of ten thousand fellowships for technological research in IITs and IISc to foster innovation. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
  • Gene Bank for Crops Germplasm: Establishment of the second Gene Bank, housing 10 lakh germplasm lines, to ensure future food and nutritional security. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Export Promotion

  • Incentives for Electronics and Electric Vehicles (EVs): Exemptions granted for open cells used in LED/LCD TVs, looms for textiles, and capital goods for lithium-ion batteries used in mobile phones and EVs to boost manufacturing and exports. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
  • Promotion of Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul (MRO): A 10-year exemption granted on goods used for shipbuilding and ships meant for breaking, along with extended time limits for the export of railway goods imported for repairs. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

New Tax Regime

  • Upto 4,00,000 tax rate will be NIL
  • From 4,00,000 to 8,00,000 tax rate will be 5%
  • From 8,00,000 to 12,00,000 tax rate will be 10%
  • From 12,00,000 to 16,00,000 tax rate will be 15%
  • From 16,00,000 to 20,00,000 tax rate will be 20%
  • From 20,00,000 to 24,00,000 tax rate will be 25%
  • Above 24,00,000 tax rate will be 30%
